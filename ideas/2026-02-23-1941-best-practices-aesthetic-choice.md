## Core Finding

Richard Prum (Yale ornithologist) argues that bird beauty evolved not for fitness signaling, but through arbitrary aesthetic choice. Sexual selection is distinct from natural selection - peacock tails persist because they're *attractive*, not because they signal superior genes. The handicap principle (costly ornaments prove fitness) is rejected. Beauty doesn't need to encode quality.

**Key insight:** Prum proposes aesthetic evolution as the **null model** - burden of proof should be on those claiming ornaments signal fitness, not those saying they're arbitrary.

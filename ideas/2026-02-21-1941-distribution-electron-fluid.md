## Core Finding from Research

**Electron behavior - Two modes:**
- **Dispersive (normal wires):** Electrons collide with impurities, lose momentum, "thud" like hacky sacks. Heat increases resistance.
- **Fluid (graphene):** Electrons collide with each other, conserve momentum like billiard balls, flow like water. Heat DECREASES resistance (Gurzhi effect).

**The difference:** Purity. Graphene has almost no impurities. Every atom in its place. This enables electrons to conserve momentum instead of dispersing it.

**Recent breakthrough (2025):** Columbia team created supersonic electron fluid shock waves using graphene shaped like rocket nozzle. Electrons exceeded their "speed of sound" (few hundred km/s).

## Selected Thesis

**Distribution as Electron Fluid: Why Purity Enables Viral Growth**

Just like graphene's atomic purity enables electrons to flow as a fluid (conserving momentum), distribution architecture's system purity enables viral growth (conserving user momentum).

**Key insight:** Every manual step, every approval gate, every "contact sales" button is an impurity in your distribution system. It turns flowing momentum (fluid) into dispersive thuds (pinball bounces).

**The parallel:**
- **Graphene:** Almost no impurities → electrons conserve momentum → electron fluid → Gurzhi effect (heat improves flow)
- **Viral products:** Almost no friction → users conserve momentum → viral growth → network effects (users improve flow)

**Anti-pattern examples:**
- Notion's "share" button requires account creation first (impurity)
- Calendly's embed requires email verification (impurity)
- Figma's invite system has permission gates (impurity)

**Pure distribution examples:**
- Loom: Watch video instantly, no signup. Momentum conserved.
- Linear's public issues: View instantly, contribute only needs account
- Vercel's deploy previews: Click link, see site, no auth

**The "heat improves flow" parallel:**
- Electron fluids: Temperature increases collision rate → MORE flow (Gurzhi effect)
- Network effects: User count increases interaction rate → MORE growth

**Quick Take hook:** "In physics, electrons in pure graphene flow like water. In product design, users in pure distribution systems flow like virality. Both fail when impurities create friction."

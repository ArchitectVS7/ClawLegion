## Selected Approach: The Hypocrisy Test

**Why this wins:**
- Exposes the real issue: not "AI bad" but "AI threatens core task"
- Cross-pollinates: priests/game devs both use tech, both reject AI for specific things
- Counter-argument format: challenges both pro-AI and anti-AI camps
- First principles: what's actually being protected? The irreducible human contribution.

**Core argument:** 
We don't object to AI because it's artificial. We object when it automates the *one thing* we think defines us.

For priests: **pastoral care** (knowing your flock)
For game devs: **creative vision** (knowing your player)
For writers: **voice** (knowing your reader)

Everything else? Fair game for automation.

**Article structure:**
1. Hook: Pope vs. game devs - same fight, different battlefield
2. The hypocrisy: everyone uses automation somewhere
3. The pattern: AI is fine until it threatens *your* irreducible task
4. The test: Ask "what's the thing only I can do?" Automate everything else.
5. Why this matters: Clarifies the AI debate - it's not about technology, it's about identity

**What makes this counter-argument:**
- Challenges pro-AI: "You can't automate everything"
- Challenges anti-AI: "You're already automating most things"
- Proposes synthesis: Protect the irreducible core, automate the periphery
